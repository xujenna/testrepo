The Fighting Game is a cooperative game in which two players team up for the ultimate quest: to resolve an interpersonal issue!

  1. Listen carefully
  2. Be honest to yourself and your partner
  3. Use "I" statements; refrain from "you"
  4. Make no assumptions
  5. Give the benefit of the doubt
  6. Refrain from bringing up past issues
  7. Refrain from narratives
  8. Avoid absolutist words, like "always" or "never"
  9. Avoid the "four horsemen": criticism, contempt, defensiveness, and stonewalling
⚠️ De-Escalate ⚠️

#1 Player 1 and 2: Accept the Challenge

Approach the game as a challenge and opportunity to communicate and understand your partner better. Maintain a positive, generous, warm, compassionate tone. Listen carefully, trust fully, be honest, empathetic, and give the benefit of the doubt.

#2 pt 1Player 1: Identify the complaint, not the criticism.

Formulate the "I" statement. Elaborate as necessary, using feeling words and avoiding the word "you", "always", and "never". Consider and include assertions of individual responsibility, self-disclosure, and empathy.

#2 pt 2Player 1: Define your emotional needs

#3 pt 1Player 2: Acknowledge Your Partner

Acknowledge and repeat the "I" statement.

#3 pt 2Player 2: Consider your responsibility

Consider and assert your responsibility, and explain your intentions/perspective. Include self-disclosure, use feeling words, and avoid using the word "you", "always", and "never".

#4 Player 1: Acknowledge their intentions

Acknowledge their intentions, consider your role in your emotions, recognize any misunderstandings, and reevaluate your feelings based on the new information given.

#5 Player 2: Propose a solution

Address the complaint and propose a solution

#6 Player 1: Renegotiate as needed

Evaluate the solution, consider your needs honestly, and if necessary, renegotiate based on your needs.

#7 Player 2: Renegotiate as needed

Repeat steps 5-6 until a solution is reached and both parties are satisfied.
